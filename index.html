Sergei Mikhailovich Eisenstein

(Russian: Сергей Михайлович Эйзенштейн, 22 January [O.S. 10 January] 1898 – 11 February 1948)

was a Soviet film director and film theorist, a pioneer in the theory and practice of montage. He is noted in particular for his silent films

  • Strike (1925),
  • Battleship Potemkin (1925)
  • and October (1928),
  • as well as the historical epics Alexander Nevsky (1938)
  • and Ivan the Terrible (1944, 1958)
